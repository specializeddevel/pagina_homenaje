Dr. René Favaloro

The Man Who Saved Humanity's Hearts

René Gerónimo Favaloro (La Plata, July 12, 1923 - Buenos Aires, July 29, 2000) was an Argentine inventor, educator, and cardiothoracic surgeon, internationally recognized for developing coronary artery bypass grafting using the great saphenous vein.

He studied medicine at the National University of La Plata, where he graduated with a thesis on ileus. Once he received his degree, he temporarily replaced the local physician in Jacinto Arauz, who was in poor health. He also studied up-to-date medical literature and developed an interest in thoracic surgery. In the late 1960s, at the Cleveland Clinic in Ohio, he began studying a technique for using the saphenous vein in coronary surgery.

In 1971, he returned to Argentina to work at the Güemes private sanatorium, thanks to his friend, interventional cardiologist Dr. Luis de la Fuente, who proposed and convinced him to come back. In the early 1970s, he founded the Favaloro Foundation, at the suggestion of Dr. De la Fuente.

Dr. Favaloro's Timeline:

  • 1923: Born in the city of La Plata, Buenos Aires on July 12.
  • 1949: Graduated as a rural doctor and served for 12 years in Jacinto Arauz, La Pampa.
  • 1962-1971: Joined the Cleveland Clinic in the United States. There, he developed microcardiac revascularization surgery or bypass technique.
  • 1975: The Favaloro Foundation was established for medical education and research.
  • 1980: Inauguration of the Basic Research Laboratory, which later became the Basic Science Research Institute of the University Institute of Biomedical Sciences.
  • 1982: The Institute of Cardiology and Cardiovascular Surgery was inaugurated.
  • 1992: In August 1992, Favaloro University was founded.
  • 2000: On July 29, 2000, he took his own life at his home in Buenos Aires by a gunshot to the heart.
